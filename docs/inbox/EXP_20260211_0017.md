# Experiment Design for Hypothesis Testing

## HYPOTHESIS
"Direct Mailers with 'Value First' message receive three times more replies compared to those without the 'Value First' messaging."

## EVIDENCE REQUIRED
- **Number of DMs Sent:** Total Direct Mail pieces sent out, split into two groups: 
  - Group A (with "Value First")
  - Group B (without "Value First")

- **Replies Received**: Count and record all replies received from both direct mailers.

## FASTEST EXPERIMENT
Conduct a small-scale test with at least:
1. Minimum of 200 DMs sent to each group.
2. Track the number of responses for an equal amount in Group A (with "Value First") as well as Group B without it over one month period, ensuring consistent demographic targeting and content aside from 'Value First'.

## METRIC
- **Response Rate Increase Factor**: Measure how much more often replies are received by comparing:
  - Response rate of DM with ValueFirst (responses per direct mail sent)
  - Standard response rate for DMs without it

An outcome showing a factor greater than or equal to `3` confirms the hypothesis.

## DECISION RULE
- **If**: The mean number of responses from Group A divided by that in Group B is â‰¥ 3, and statistical testing (like Chi-Square test) shows significance.
- **Then Y**: Accept H5 ("Value First" DMs get three times more replies).

### Statistical Test to Apply:
Use a Two-Proportion Z-Test for the observed response rates of both groups.
