# Experimental Design for Testing H1

## Hypothesis
H1: Aggressive copy converts better than polite copy by at least 20%.

## Evidence Required 
To test this hypothesis effectively:
- Conversion rates (percentage of users who click on a link or make some form of desired action) from aggressive copies versus polite copies.
  
**Data Points Needed**
1. Number of impressions for both aggressive and polite versions.
2. Click-through rate (CTR) to measure conversion success.

## Fastest Experiment
To achieve 80% confidence with the smallest test:
- Conduct an A/B testing campaign where you show equal numbers of users either version over a short period, e.g., one week on popular websites or social media platforms like Facebook ads.
  
**Sample Size Calculation**
Using power analysis for comparing two means (aggressive vs. polite), assuming normal distribution and aiming to achieve 80% confidence level:
- t-test with desired effect size of at least 20%.
